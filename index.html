The objective of the analysis was to build a model able to classify in five different classes: “A”, “B”, “C”, “D”, “E” the quality of execution of a specific physical exercise: weight lifting. The data were collected using several motion sensors such as accelerometers, gyroscope located at different upper body parts. A training dataset and an indipendent test dataset were provided to develop and validate the model accuracy.

The training data included 19622 observations and each observation was composed by 160 variables. Variables identified user and time and which were missing in more then the 90% of the observation were discarded because not provide useful information for the model development.

After the initial cleaning 53 predictors out of 160 were kept for the analysis. The following step included the division of the initial training dataset in training, 70% of the data, used for tunes the model parameters, choose the predictors and choose the best classifier, and testing, 30% of the data to provide an estimation of out of sample error.

By plotting the histogram of the targe variable, classe of the training data allowed to evaluate the balance of data.

In order to keep only the most useful information, the predictors which showed low variance in the training set and would have not contribute to distinguish different classes were discarded from both training and test set. 

# Identify and eliminate predictors with zero variance
nsv <- nearZeroVar(training, saveMetrics = TRUE)

To visualize the information content of the selected features, principal component analysis (PCA) decomposition were performed on the training data. The principal componenents which explained an overall explained variance equal to the 90% were kept. The PCA allowed projecting the selected features into a two-dimensional space and visualizing the their spread per classes (see belove). The variance retained by the first two principal components was around 30%. 

# PCA with explained variance of 90% 
num_pred <- sapply(training, is.numeric)
object<-preProcess(training[,num_pred], method = "pca", thresh = 0.90)
comp<-predict(object,training[,num_pred])
qplot(comp[,1],comp[,2],color = training$classe, xlab = "PC1", ylab = "PC2")
